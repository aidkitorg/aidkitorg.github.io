WELCOME AND OVERVIEW

Hello! Welcome to the Thriving Providers Project participant consent form. The purpose of this form is to share with you important information and considerations about participating in the program and receiving cash payments over a 18-month period.

You will want to understand all aspects of this form before deciding whether you want to participate. Please ask questions. If you decide to participate, you will sign the form at the end. This is a legally-binding document.

ABOUT THE PROGRAM

Program Title: Thriving Providers Project (“The Program”)

Program Duration: 18 months

Thriving Providers Project: The program will provide a monthly cash payment to family, friend, and neighbor child care providers with the goal of supporting the economic well-being of providers. The program is led by Impact Charitable, a non-profit organization 501(c)3, in partnership with local community-based organizations. We also work with AidKit, which is the technology platform we use to complete your applications and distribute the cash assistance. Our three organizations work together to make this project possible!

Program Participation: As a potential participant in the program, it is important that you read the description of the program and ask questions so you understand what to expect if you decide to participate. Please consider the information carefully to make sure the program is a good fit and to understand the possible impacts participation may have on you.

Participant Agreements: You agree that...

  • All the information you provided to us in this application (including your identity, children under your care, your relationship with those children, and the hours of care provided per week, etc.) will be accurate, true and complete to the best of your knowledge.
  • You are caring for no more than four children at any given time, or two children under two years of age, unless all children are from the same family.
  • You intend to provide childcare services to at least one child who is not your own (non-custodial, non-biological child) for the next 18 months. If this changes, you agree to inform the program.
  • You reside in the State of Colorado.
  • You have no relationship (i.e. you are a family member through blood or marriage) with any member of Impact Charitable.

PROGRAM SERVICES

Program Services: The program will provide the following services for a 18-month period:

  1. Cash Assistance Payments ($500 per month for 18 months)
  2. Optional Mental Health Services (optional)
  3. Optional Resource Navigation & Referral (optional)

For more details see Program Overview detailed below.

OTHER PROGRAM TERMS

Confidentiality

All of the information you provide will be kept confidential and stored in an encrypted, secure data-base. Your name will not be used in any report, and any program data reports will be reported in a way that will not identify you.

No Risk of Public Charge

There is no public charge risk applying for or participating in this program.

Confirmation & Pause of Payments

We will do our best to make sure that all cash payments are reaching you in full. Therefore, on a periodic basis, the Thriving Providers Project may reach out to you to confirm you are receiving the cash payments. We will contact you though the contact information you provided to us on your application (email or phone). If we cannot reach you, the Thriving Providers Project reserves the right to pause payments to make sure they are not being sent to an incorrect recipient. If we pause payments, all payments will be delivered once we get in touch with you and confirm you have been receiving the payments. After this confirmation, the pause will be lifted. This helps make sure you get all of the cash assistance you are supposed to.

Termination

This program is designed to support the well-being of child care providers and the families they care for. As such, we have the right to terminate your enrollment in this program. Specifically, your participation may be terminated for the following reasons:

  • If you no longer meet the eligibility requirements during re-verification (every 6 months)
  • If you voluntarily withdraw from the program (see below);
  • If you knowingly misrepresent your identity or eligibility to project staff;
  • If you commit violence or make threats of violence to any project staff, including threats made over the phone, email, text message, or in person;
  • If The Thriving Providers Project determines that your participation in the program has caused harm to others; or
  • If you are deceased

Termination from the program means you will no longer have access to the monthly cash payments. You will be notified in writing by the Thriving Providers Project if your participation is terminated.

Additionally, if you choose to be paid by prepaid debit card (see below for more information on the payment options) and you do not activate your card within thirty (30) days of receiving it, the Thriving Providers Project can cancel the payment and terminate you from the program to provide other providers the opportunity to join. We will attempt to contact you using the contact information you provided at least one time prior to cancelling the payment, and will inform the community-based organization who enrolled you to support you in activating the prepaid debit card if you need assistance.

Voluntary Withdraw

You may choose to withdraw from at any time by notifying the Thriving Providers Project in writing. You can inform us by email (thrivingproviders@impactcharitable.com) or by written letter to our address (Impact Charitable, 1536 Wynkoop St., Suite 223 Denver, CO 80202). Once you withdraw from the program, you cannot be reinstated into the program. Before we end your payments, we will attempt to reach you by phone or in writing to confirm your withdrawal.

Grievances

You can also call by phone at 720-500-5635. All appeal requests will be reviewed and participants will be notified of the outcomes.

As we work together to make this program work, you understand that if things go wrong (including if you or someone else is harmed), the program, including the program's sponsor, Impact Charitable, community-based organizations partnered on the program, and all of the staff, volunteers, directors, and officers of these organizations and this program are not responsible for any damages or legal or financial claims of any kind related to you or anyone harmed by your participation in the program.

THRIVING PROVIDERS PROJECT PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Congratulations on being a part of The Thriving Providers Project! You will be given a monthly cash payment because we know the work you do caring for children is important. It is our goal to support the economic well-being of family, friend, and neighbor child care providers

PROGRAM OVERVIEW

Payment Amount: You will receive $250 every two weeks, for a total of $500 per month.

Payment Duration: You will receive 18 months of payments.

Payment Method: You have two options for how you'd like to receive payments:

  • Option 1: Direct Bank Deposit:

    Payments will be sent through a direct bank deposit (ACH transfer) to the participant's bank account. The participant has to have an active bank account in the United States to select this method.

  • Option 2: Debit Card:

    The program will provide the applicant with a USIO debit card. The program will load the payments to the participant's debit card. The participant does NOT need a bank account to receive payments using this method.

Payment Frequency: You will receive $250 at the beginning of the month and $250 in the middle of the month. The exact dates for when you will receive your payments is dependent on which payment method you chose.

  • Direct Bank Deposit:

    All monthly cash assistance payments will be sent on the 12th & 28th of the month, and will likely hit the participants bank account around the 1st and 15th of the month. Please note, given direct bank deposits can take 2-3 days, we cannot predict exactly when the recipient will receive the payments.

  • Debit Card:

    All monthly cash assistance payments will be loaded onto the participants debit card on the 1st and 15th of the month.

Peer Support Groups

You will be given the option to join a peer support group. This group will meet weekly. If you choose to join, you will be placed in a group with other providers in your area. The weekly meetings will be led by a trained clinician with experience providing mental health services. In the meeting, you can discuss a variety of topics (i.e. how to support children with mental health, emotional, and social development, or how to deal with stress, burnout, anxiety or other needs). In the meetings, you will also learn about upcoming trainings, events, or other resources that may be helpful to you. Groups will be offered in Spanish or English, depending on what language you prefer.

These peer support groups will take place via Zoom (on smartphone, tablet or computer) or by phone (if internet is not available). You do not have to attend every weekly meeting.

This is an optional activity and not required by participants in the Thriving Providers Project.

Resource Referral & Navigation

If you are also interested in learning about resources in your area can also be connected with a Peer Navigator. Peer Navigators will help you find locally available resources in their community, like professional development opportunities, food banks, local library events, and more.

If you choose, you will be added to a WhatsApp, SMS, or Facebook group to receive updates, connect with other providers, and ask for support in finding resources.

This is an optional activity and not required by participants in the Thriving Providers Project.

ELIGIBILITY CRITERIA

This program is intended for family, friends, and neighbor child care providers currently caring for children that are not your own. While you are now approved and enrolled in the program, we will need to have your eligibility reverified every six months. During this reverification, we will verify whether:

  1. You still caring for at least one non-custodial, non-biological child, and
  2. You are caring for less than four children at any given time or less than two children under two at any time (unless you have a child care license or are caring for a single family).

To verify, we will contact you every six months using the contact information you provided on your application.

If we cannot get in touch with you, or you are no longer eligible, you will have 90 days to meet the re-verification requirements or the cash payments will end. We understand that in some cases, the families you provided child care services to at the start of the program move or no longer require services. In this case, you will need to find a new family to care for in order to remain enrolled in the program within 90 days of the reverification period.

PUBLIC BENEFITS

It important that you understand you run the risk of experiencing a loss or decrease in public benefits (i.e. SNAP, WIC, etc.), given the monthly cash payment associated with participating in this program. Upon enrolling, you will receive education around your benefits to help you understand the potential impacts of program involvement on cash and non-cash benefits. We can help provide resources and support to understand the risks, as needed. You, however, are responsible for ensuring you understand the impact and are comfortable with joining the program prior to signing the Thriving Providers Project agreement form.

COMMONLY ASKED QUESTIONS

Can I change how I'd like to receive payments?

Yes, you can change the payment method or bank account at any time during the program period. To do so, contact the Community Based Organization (CBO) whom enrolled you (contact information below).

I have a new phone number or my contact information changed. What should I do?

If you would like to change your contact information, you must notify the Community Based Organization (CBO) that enrolled you. Your phone number will be used to re verify your eligibility. To continue to receive payments, we must be able to get ahold of you so please keep us up to date as your contact information changes.

How do I activate my USIO debit card?

You can activate your card by calling the number on the sticker on the card. This is easy - they will ask you to set your PIN number once activate. Don't share this PIN number with anyone!

How do I find my balance on my USIO debit card?

You can find your balance by calling USIO at 1-855-925-4626. You can also create an online account at Akimbo Card and see your balance anytime through the online portal. The community-based organization that enrolled you will not be able to tell you your balance.

How do I get a new USIO debit card if I lost mine or it was stolen?

If a card is lost or stolen, you must:

  • Report the card as lost/stolen to the USIO customer support team as soon as possible by calling 1-855-925-4626
  • The USIO customer support team can mail you a card (this takes up to 30 days) or you can contact the community-based organization that enrolled you to get a new debit card issued and activated after the card has been reported as stolen.

Can I get reimbursed for the money that was used off my stolen USIO debit card?

The program will only load the amount equal to the remaining balance from the old card onto your new card. If there are any funds that were stolen/used before the card is reported stolen to USIO, the applicant will need to dispute charges directly with USIO by calling 1-855-925-4626

Where can I use my USIO debit card?

You can use your card anywhere that accepts Mastercard as well as any ATM. It works just like a normal debit card would.

Can I use an ATM to withdraw cash using my USIO debit card?

Yes. You can use an ATM to withdraw cash using your card. You can find a list of ATMs at All Point Network. If you use these ATMs, you will be charged a $2 fee. If you use another ATM, you will be charged a $2 fee plus any other fees company may charge for using their ATM (usually $2-$5). However, if you select the 'Cash Back' option when you are checking out at a supermarket and paying with your debit card, you won't be charged any fee to withdraw cash! This is the cheapest and best option. To do this, just use your debit card when you buy groceries and when checking out, select the option to 'Get Cash Back' or inform the cashier you want cash back.

Will I have to pay taxes on this income?

No. This income is a gift[1] as defined by the IRS and is therefore not taxable income. As such, you do not need to claim it as income for your tax returns.

[1] A gift is a designation by the IRS. See 26 USC §102 (Gifts and Inheritance); IRS Rev. Rul. 99-44 (Gifts and Charitable Contributions). If an individual receives a gift, they qualify for the annual gift tax exclusion, meaning the income is non-taxable income.
